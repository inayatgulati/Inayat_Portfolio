My Journey as a Designer

2022-2023

The Perfect Combination

Discovered UX/UI design was the perfect blend of what I love—creativity meets research and continuous learning. What excites me most isn't just making things look good, but understanding why people interact with products the way they do, then using that insight to create something better. Every project became an opportunity to learn something new.

2023-2024

Real Users, Real Impact

The shift from classroom concepts to working with actual users changed everything. Talking with real people, understanding their frustrations, and then building something that genuinely helps them—that's when design clicked for me. Learned that the best solutions aren't the ones with the most features, but the ones that feel simple and intuitive, even when there's complexity happening behind the scenes.

Summer 2025

Simplifying the Complex

MasterCard internship reinforced what I believe: users shouldn't have to see or understand the backend complexity—they should just experience ease. Learned that truly great UX is invisible. The harder challenge isn't adding features, but knowing what to remove and how to make powerful functionality feel effortless. Also discovered the value of collaborating with other designers—hearing different perspectives always leads to better solutions.

Fall 2025

Building for People, Not Portfolios

Leading the complete design of a travel app reminded me why I do this work: creating tools people actually want to use, not just admire. My approach now is clear—deeply understand user needs, collaborate with others to challenge my assumptions, then design experiences that feel so simple and natural that users don't think about the interface at all. When tech feels easy, that's when we've done our job right.
