Workplace Experience SharePoint

Building a Centralized, Accessible User Guide for Mastercard's Global Workforce

Project Overview

During my UX/UI internship at Mastercard's Vancouver Tech Hub, I worked within the Workplace Experience Department under the guidance of the Vice President of Workplace Experience. My main focus was to redesign the internal SharePoint platform—transforming it from a static information repository into a comprehensive user guide system for employees across Mastercard's global offices.

This redesign aimed to make the platform more intuitive, engaging, and accessible—especially for employees visiting or transferring to the Vancouver branch from other global offices. It consolidated everything from office policies, catering services, and facility booking forms to travel information and event resources, ensuring that every employee could easily navigate workplace processes without friction.

Problem

The existing Workplace Experience SharePoint served primarily as an information archive—lacking the structure, visual clarity, and discoverability employees needed.

Employees, especially those travelling to or newly joining the Vancouver branch, often struggled to locate essential resources such as catering forms, IT requests, event bookings, and building information. This led to communication bottlenecks, repeated support emails, and low engagement with the platform.

Design Challenge

"How might we redesign the Workplace Experience SharePoint into an accessible, user-centric guide that simplifies onboarding, supports cross-branch employees, and fosters a cohesive workplace experience?"

Discovery & Research

To deeply understand employee needs, I conducted qualitative interviews and workplace observations, and developed personas and journey maps representing different employee groups—from local staff to visiting teams from Dublin, India, and the U.S.

Insight 1

Inconsistent Navigation: Employees couldn't find important forms or resources quickly.

Insight 2

Lack of Context: Visiting employees often felt uncertain about local services and workspace processes.

Insight 3

Outdated Layout: The structure didn't reflect how people actually interact with information daily.

By mapping out employee journeys, I identified the most common tasks—such as booking meeting rooms, requesting catering, and accessing workplace policies—and restructured the SharePoint to prioritize these workflows.

Ideation and Design Process

Collaborating with the Vice President and Project Manager, I synthesized my research findings and journey map insights into clear, actionable design directions. Additionally, I worked closely with teams in Dublin, India, and the U.S. to ensure the redesign could scale globally while maintaining regional flexibility and content relevance.

Decision 1: Modular Homepage with Side Navigation

To make the site intuitive and efficient, I designed a side navigation menu structured as an organized list of key categories, giving employees immediate access to critical information without excessive scrolling or hidden pages.

The menu included sections such as:

  • Home
  • Visiting Vancouver
  • Transportation
  • Amenities
  • Events
  • Teams in Vancouver
  • Floor plans
  • Access Card Info
  • Storage & Parking Info
  • Forms and Policies

This structured menu replaced the previous scattered layout, allowing users—especially those new or visiting—to find what they needed within seconds.

Decision 2: Information Architecture Overhaul

Guided by journey mapping insights, I completely restructured the information hierarchy to reduce redundancies and improve clarity. Pages were reorganized based on user goals rather than departmental ownership, resulting in new intuitive categories such as "Plan Your Visit," "Book a Space," and "Workplace Support."

This approach simplified the content flow and reflected how employees naturally searched for workplace information, making the platform both task-oriented and discoverable.

Decision 3: Visual Identity + Accessibility Enhancements

To create a consistent and visually engaging experience, I established a refined color and layout system that balanced Mastercard's corporate branding with functional clarity.

  • Primary Colors: Black and off-white accents were used for sub-categories to visually separate different content views.
  • Functional Colors: Black was reserved for all interactive buttons, ensuring strong contrast and accessibility compliance.
  • Status Colors: Muted grey for Circulars and bright orange for Notices maintained clear visual differentiation.

These color-coded systems enhanced scannability, guided user attention, and brought brand consistency to every page.

Decision 4: Global Scalability + Consistency

After piloting the redesigned SharePoint in Vancouver, I collaborated with Workplace Experience teams in Dublin, India, and the U.S. to adapt the new structure to their local branches. This involved remote design reviews, asynchronous testing, and continuous iteration based on feedback from each region.

The final framework was flexible enough to accommodate local content while maintaining consistent information architecture and design language across all global instances—ensuring a unified Mastercard experience for every employee.

Collaboration and Implementation

Working as the only UX/UI designer in the department, I led the end-to-end design process and maintained biweekly design syncs with the Vice President and Project Manager to align on progress and decisions.

Given SharePoint's technical limitations, I partnered directly with IT support to translate design specifications into feasible modules. My iterative prototyping approach ensured that visual quality and usability were never compromised despite system constraints.

Outcome and Impact

The redesigned Workplace Experience SharePoint significantly improved usability and engagement across Mastercard's internal teams:

Simplified Access

Employees could locate key resources up to 40% faster through reorganized navigation and visual cues.

Global Adoption

The structure and design principles were later adopted by multiple Mastercard branches for their local SharePoints.

Enhanced Employee Experience

Visiting employees reported a smoother onboarding experience when transitioning to the Vancouver office.

Increased Internal Visibility

The Workplace Experience Department gained greater recognition for its clarity and communication efficiency.

What I Learned

Designing for Global Teams

Strategic Thinking

Journey mapping and persona development helped me tie UX decisions directly to employee pain points and organizational goals.

Adaptability in Constraints

Designing within SharePoint's framework taught me to balance creativity with feasibility—an essential skill for enterprise UX.

Leadership in Collaboration

As the only designer, I strengthened my ability to communicate, advocate, and make informed design decisions confidently.
