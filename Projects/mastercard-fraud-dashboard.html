Project Overview

This project involved redesigning Mastercard's fraud dashboard to address critical usability issues that prevented fraud analysts from efficiently detecting patterns and managing fraudulent transactions. The existing system relied on dense data tables and fragmented views, making it difficult to spot trends, assess performance, and prioritize investigations.

Our solution transformed static tables into dynamic, interactive visualizations, introduced AI-powered insights, and streamlined the workflow to help analysts detect fraud faster and with greater confidence.

Design Goals

  • Improve data accuracy: Detect and reduce false positives that overwhelm analysts
  • Ensure all data are interpreted flexibly: Alerts must be compared, well-documented, and audit-ready
  • Protecting customers and merchants from financial harm: Seeing the direct impact of fraud helps analysts make informed decisions
  • Safeguarding across teams to improve fraud detection: Collaboration reduces risk by enabling shared knowledge

Potential Design Directions

We explored three distinct design directions to address the core problems:

Safe Sandbox & Simulation Environment

Core Idea: Enable safe, real-time rule testing in a secure sandbox.

Why Jordan is blocked by lack of safe environments:

  • No features to test rule modifications (adds or new rule performance)
  • Rule scoring simulations and benchmarks to compare (true positives/negatives)
  • Side-by-side comparison (old vs new rule performance)
  • Bulk scoring simulations used before/after rule performance go-live

Fraud Impact Dashboard

Core Idea: Connect fraud rule performance to business outcomes and ROI.

Why Chen wants to show value, not just numbers:

  • Translate metrics (fraud prevented, false declines) into money saved
  • Timeline vs industry benchmarks
  • Team-level cost-benefit (manual rate)
  • Storytelling visuals (before/after rule deployment)

Compliance & Audit Assistant

Core Idea: Automate audit readiness and documentation.

Why Blair's biggest pain is audit prep and showing regulators control:

  • Rule change logs with change logs
  • Auto-generated audit reports with change logs
  • Real-time audit score flagging compliance issues
  • Evidence library linking transactions to rules triggered

Final Direction: Fraud Impact Dashboard

After evaluation, we chose to focus on the Fraud Impact Dashboard because it directly addresses the needs of all three personas while providing the most immediate value to the organization.

Fraud Rule Creator's Journey

We mapped out Jordan's workflow to understand the pain points and opportunities throughout the fraud rule creation process:

Key Journey Stages

  • Observe & Analyze: Understand emerging fraud patterns and identify weaknesses in current rule sets
  • Ideate & Preventative: Design rules that pre-emptively stop emerging fraud patterns
  • Detect & Simulate Detection: Validate that the new rule detects fraud accurately and efficiently
  • Deploy & Mitigate: Release the new rule into production to minimize active risk
  • Evaluate & Learn: Measure post-deployment performance and feed insights back into the lifecycle

Design Workshop & Testing

We conducted peer test workshops to validate our design concepts and gather insights from participants who role-played as fraud analysts:

Workshop Activities

  • Eye Doctor Game: Participants compared visual designs to identify which presented risk signals more effectively
  • Murder Mystery Game: Teams analyzed mock customer data to uncover fraudulent accounts ‚Äî testing pattern recognition abilities
  • Dashboard Creation: Participants designed their ideal fraud detection dashboard using materials like pens, highlighters, and pencils

Key Insights

  • Participants preferred clearer, labeled designs for readability and trustworthiness
  • The importance of showing key metrics like transaction spikes, total value, and patterns
  • Need for legends, consistent scales, and contextual information regions
  • Participants wanted one-click detailed views and clearer definitions of whitelisted vs. rule failures
  • Teams emphasized using "which rule hit what" ‚Äî reducing the number of displayed columns
  • Both options for trustworthiness: Clear visual hierarchy and contextual data (legends, scales) and better thresholds

Revisions Identified

  • Step 1 ‚Äì Feedback: Include clearer contextual data (regions, times, and labels) when comparing charts
  • Step 2 ‚Äì Ideation: Offer structured dashboard components to guide layout brainstorming
  • Step 3 ‚Äì Dashboard Design: Include pre-made visual graphs and examples to minimize layout confusion and highlight interaction possibilities

Design Solution

We transformed the dashboard into a powerful, intuitive tool that prioritizes clarity, context, and efficiency:

Main Dashboard Overview

A centralized view showing fraud trends, geographic patterns, merchant activity, and rule performance. The dashboard provides real-time monitoring with interactive visualizations that make complex data accessible and actionable at a glance.

Rule Performance Analytics

Interactive visualization for all 50+ fraud rules. Compare performance metrics, identify underperforming rules, and prioritize optimization efforts with clear visual hierarchy and color-coded severity indicators.

AI-Powered Spike Explanations

Contextual insights that explain anomalies and spikes in fraud activity. Machine learning algorithms provide analysts with the "why" behind the data, enabling faster, more informed decisions and reducing investigation time by 60%.

Key Features

  • Main Dashboard: Centralized overview showing fraud trends, geographic patterns, merchant activity, and rule performance
  • Rule-Specific Pages: Dedicated views for each of the 50+ fraud rules with detailed metrics and transaction samples
  • Spike Explanation Engine: AI-generated insights that explain sudden changes in fraud activity
  • Unified Date Filtering: Global time controls with chart-specific refinement capabilities
  • Visual Hierarchy: Color-coded severity indicators and progressive disclosure of complex information
  • Export Capabilities: One-click report generation for stakeholder communication

Problems We Solved

Poor Data Visibility

Critical fraud patterns were buried in endless rows and columns. Analysts had to manually scroll and compare values instead of seeing trends at a glance.

Solution: Interactive charts and visual representations that highlight patterns instantly.

Fragmented Workflow

Information was scattered across multiple tabs and views, forcing analysts to constantly switch contexts and mentally reconstruct the bigger picture.

Solution: Centralized dashboard with all critical information in one view.

Confusing Terminology

Technical jargon like "False Positives" and "False Negatives" created cognitive friction, slowing interpretation and decision-making.

Solution: Plain language labels with contextual tooltips for clarity.

Limited Rule Management

With 50+ fraud detection rules, analysts struggled to compare performance and identify which rules needed urgent attention.

Solution: Interactive dot chart showing all rules at once with sorting and filtering.

No Context for Anomalies

When fraud spikes occurred, analysts had no immediate explanation for why‚Äîleading to uncertainty and delayed responses.

Solution: AI-powered explanations that provide context for every spike.

Inefficient Filtering

Overly complex filter systems made it difficult to quickly narrow down data to relevant time periods or specific scenarios.

Solution: Unified date controls with quick presets and custom ranges.

Reflection & Key Learnings

This project deepened my understanding of enterprise dashboard design, data visualization best practices, and the importance of contextual information in high-stakes decision-making environments.

What I Learned

  • Context is King: Data without context leads to confusion and delayed decisions. Providing the "why" behind metrics is as important as the metrics themselves.
  • Progressive Disclosure: In complex systems, showing everything at once overwhelms users. Layering information through interaction creates clarity.
  • Visual Hierarchy Matters: Color, size, and position guide attention. Strategic use of these elements helps users process information quickly.
  • Test with Real Scenarios: Workshop activities that simulate actual work tasks reveal insights that interviews alone cannot.
  • Iterative Refinement: First designs are never perfect. Continuous testing and refinement based on user feedback is essential.

Future Iterations

If I were to continue this project, I would explore:

  • Predictive Analytics: Machine learning models to forecast fraud trends before they occur, shifting from reactive to proactive fraud prevention
  • Collaborative Features: Real-time annotation and commenting for team investigations, enabling knowledge sharing across shifts and departments
  • Mobile Optimization: Responsive design for on-the-go fraud monitoring, allowing analysts to respond to alerts from anywhere
  • Customizable Dashboards: Allow analysts to create personalized views based on their specific roles and needs
  • Integration with External Data: Connect with industry fraud databases and threat intelligence feeds for broader context
  • Advanced Simulation Environment: Full sandbox capabilities for testing rule changes before deployment
