Sculpture Pavilion

Spatial Design: A Conceptual Architectural Exhibition Space

Timeline

Feb '24 - Mar '24

Project Type

Solo Architectural Design

Software

Rhino 3D, Adobe Creative Suite

Overview

The Sculpture Pavilion is a conceptual architectural design that serves as an interactive exhibition space for sculptures. The project focuses on layered panelling, spatial hierarchy, and dynamic light interactions, offering visitors a unique sensory experience as they navigate the pavilion.

Project Goals

  • Create an immersive, multi-level exhibition space for sculpture displays
  • Integrate light and shadow dynamics to enhance spatial engagement
  • Design clear navigation pathways to ensure fluid visitor movement
  • Apply layered architecture principles to balance aesthetics with functionality

Challenges & Solutions

Challenge 1

Ensuring fluid navigation through an open space

Solution: Defined pathways using layered panels and clear entry/exit points

Challenge 2

Balancing structural integrity with an open design

Solution: Used staggered and intersecting panels to maintain form while enhancing openness

Challenge 3

Maximizing light interaction without overwhelming the space

Solution: Placed voids in key areas to allow controlled natural light penetration

Design Process

Step 1: Initial Sketch & Conceptualization

The initial design envisioned a two-story structure with an inclined roof, emphasizing symmetry and balance. However, early explorations in Rhino revealed the potential for enhancing spatial generosity and refining movement pathways.

Step 2: Refining Structure & Layout

Using Rules 1 and 3 of layered architecture, panels were designed to:

  • Overlap and intersect, creating depth and rhythm
  • Open voids for natural light penetration and visual contrast
  • Establish a structured yet flexible navigation system for visitors

Step 3: Circulation & Interaction Design

Dual staircases offer two movement pathways:

  • Visitors can ascend from the right, following a structured route through sculptures
  • Alternatively, they can start at the upper level, enjoying a bird's-eye view before descending

Step 4: Light & Shadow Experimentation

By positioning panels at strategic angles, the design creates a rhythmic interplay of light and shadow, dynamically shifting throughout the day. This enhances the sculptures' visual prominence while providing a changing visitor experience.

Final Sketches & Digital Views

Rendered Perspective

Examines how natural light filters through the layers, creating a dynamic spatial experience.

Final Outcome

  • A visually engaging pavilion that seamlessly blends art with architecture
  • A multi-pathway exploration experience allowing visitors to engage with sculptures from different perspectives
  • A rhythmic, light-sensitive environment where movement through the space changes how art is perceived

Key Takeaways

Spatial Hierarchy and Layered Architecture

Enhance both function and form through thoughtful organization of space and materials.

Interactivity Through Circulation

Perspective shifts improve visitor engagement and create dynamic experiences.

Light as an Architectural Element

Can elevate a space from static to dynamic, transforming how visitors perceive art.
